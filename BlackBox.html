Black Box Investigation

Introduction

Mr. Sinusas’s mystery black box contains a hidden mechanism. When we pour in 1,000 ml of water, the output sometimes increases or decreases. We tested this in a controlled setting by pouring in 1,000 ml and measuring the output. Our goal was to form a hypothesis about what was inside the box and gather data.

Claim

Inside the Black Box, there is a split tube that leads into two different containers. When we input water, the output varies, suggesting the water is being divided. Our early data showed that after several pours, the box even produced extra output.

Evidence

My group brainstormed possible internal mechanisms. In my model, the tube did not split correctly and there were not enough containers, so it didn’t function as expected. After a certain amount of water, the tube only output what was put in. The key structures were a tipping bucket and a tube feeding into it. We tested by pouring in 100 ml at a time.

Reasoning

Models help us visualize or test ideas. They are useful because they can replicate the original system, but they also have limitations. My claim is correct because a single tube cannot produce two different outputs without splitting. Even though my model didn’t work perfectly, it showed that a single tube cannot split water on its own.

Photography

We used cameras to document our progress using techniques like rule of thirds, framing, and angle variation. These photos helped us track our design evolution.

Photoshop

We used Photoshop to illustrate our ideas and animate the water flow using frame-by-frame animation. We also created GIFs to visualize the process.

Maya

Using Maya, we built a 3D model of the Black Box. We used tools like extrude, bevel, curves, booleans, and lighting. We also modeled hands, joints, tubes, supports, and funnels.
