Our Shared Canvas

Introduction

The project was all about using art to send a message and fix a problem in our community. We worked with screenprinting to create art pieces that addressed an issue in our community. For this project I wanted to use art to help students with procrastination. Procrastination is something many students struggle with and often don’t fully understand. My goal was to show how procrastination works, and to motivate people to think about their own habits. To do this, I created a screen print based on my research and documented the entire process in a video.

Reasearch and Selecting my Issue

I started by researching common workplace issues and creating a list of possible topics. I chose procrastination because it is a major problem for students, who were my target audience. To better understand the issue, I researched it in several ways and built a loopy 5 whys dynamic systems model to see how my artwork could influence student behavior.

Photography and Initial Design

After choosing my concept, I sketched a rough design and asked my teammates to take photos of me posing in that position. I reviewed all the photos and selected the one that best matched my idea. This became the base image for my artwork.

Editing in Photoshop

In photoshop, I removed the background from my chosen photo and converted it to black and white using tools like Threshold and Rasterize. I cleaned up any rough edges with the photo restoration filter and spot healing brush, then added a white border. I also created custom warped text that said “procrastination” to highlight the theme.

Planning in Illustrator

Next, I used Adobe Illustrator to create a dimensional diagram, a cut list, and a color‑mixing guide. These plans helped me map out exactly how I would cut and assemble the wood for my screen‑printing frame.

Building the Frame

Once the plans were ready, I measured, marked, and cut each piece of wood. I glued the pieces together, stapled them, and hammered the staples to make sure the frame was strong and secure. I stretched mesh tightly over the frame, stapled it down, and hammered the staples so the surface stayed smooth. This created the base for the screen‑printing stencil.

Creating the Vinyl Stencil

Using Silhouette Studio, I sent my digital design to a Cricut machine, which cut a vinyl stencil that matched my artwork. I ironed this stencil onto the mesh so I could begin the printing process.

Screen Printing the Artwork

To prepare the screen, I taped off all areas not covered by vinyl to create a clean stencil. I added black acrylic paint along the top edge and used a squeegee to pull the paint across with firm, even pressure. This pushed the paint through the mesh and transferred my design onto the material below.

21st Century Skill Reflection

One of the 21st Century skills that I demonstrated during this project was my accessing and analyzing information skills. I had to use this skill a lot because before this quarter I had no idea how to use any of the tools used in our projects. I had to not only take advice from teachers and peers but also research how to use tools on my own time so I could advise my friends and classmates. For example in the Maya unit I wanted to know how to add a cloth in my render for one of the “do nows”. Because the teachers were wrapped up with other students I was able to research how to do it on my own by using multiple research tools from different sources such as YouTube and informative websites. During the project I was also able to accurately take the information from the teachers and use it in my work. One example of how I did this was in our Photoshop unit I was able to listen to the teacher and figure out how to use different brushes.
